George Washington: Tribute Page

George Washington

Founding Father of the United States

Here's a time line of George Washington's life:

  • 1732 - Born in Popes Creek Plantation, Westmoreland County
  • 1743 - George Washington's father, Augustine Washington died
  • 1751 - Takes a trip to a Carribean island called Barbados with his half-brother Lawrence who was sicked with a respiratory illness
  • 1752 - George's half brother, Lawrence Washington dies
  • 1753 - Is sent to assess the strength of the French in the Ohio Valley
  • 1754 - Lieutenant Colonel Washington successfully attacks the French camp at Fort Dunquessne
  • 1758 - Was elected to represent Frederick County in the Virginia House of Burgesses
  • 1759 - Marries to Martha Custis and move to Mount Vernon
  • 1761 - Becomes the owner of Mount Vernon after his sister-in-law dies
  • 1766 - Stop growing tobacco as crop on his farms when he realized that it used up many of the nutrients in the soil
  • 1775 - Given the title Command in Chief and Was chosen to lead the army during the American Revolution against the British Army
  • 1776 - Led his troops on a secret mission to cross the icy Delaware River to attack soldiers fighting for England
  • 1778 - Washington and his soldiers spent a very cold winter in a camp called Valley Forge with not enough food, clothing, or firewood
  • 1783 - After winning the American Revolution, he left his job in the army and went back home to Mount Vernon
  • 1789 - Becomes the first President of the United States of America
  • 1791 - Took a trip to the southern states to meet people who lived in the United States
  • 1792 - Was chosen to be President for the second time
  • 1797 - Retires presidency
  • 1799 - George Washington dies at 67 years old and buried at Mount Vernon
